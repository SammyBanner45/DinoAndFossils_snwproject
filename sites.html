Dinosaurs: Giants of the Mesozoic Era


The eras of the Colossals:
Era Period Time Span (Million Years Ago) Key Features
Mesozoic Triassic 252 - 201 First dinosaurs and mammals appeared; dry climate with deserts.
Mesozoic Jurassic 201 - 145 Age of giant sauropods; lush vegetation; first birds evolved.
Mesozoic Cretaceous 145 - 66 Flowering plants appeared; T. rex and Triceratops roamed; ended with mass extinction.

Hell Creek Formation (USA)

One of the richest dinosaur fossil sites in North America, famous for T. rex, Triceratops, and fossils marking the end of the Cretaceous period.

Morrison Formation (USA)

This Late Jurassic site is known for its abundant sauropod fossils like Diplodocus and Apatosaurus, revealing life in prehistoric floodplains of North Colorado.

Liaoning Fossil Beds (China)

Renowned for exceptionally preserved feathered dinosaurs, birds, and early mammals — a snapshot of the transition between dinosaurs and birds.

Gobi Desert (Mongolia)

One of the most important dinosaur fossil regions on Earth, home to Velociraptor, Protoceratops, and preserved dinosaur eggs in the Lonesome lands of Mongolia.

Tendaguru Beds (Tanzania)

One of Africa's most significant fossil sites, containing Jurassic giants like Brachiosaurus and Kentrosaurus, comparable to U.S. Morrison Formation.

Ischigualasto Formation (Argentina)

Known as the “Valley of the Moon,” this Triassic site contains some of the earliest known dinosaurs, offering insight into their early evolution.
