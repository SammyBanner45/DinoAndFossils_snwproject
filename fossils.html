Dinosaurs: Giants of the Mesozoic Era


Dinosaurs are a diverse group of reptiles of the clade Dinosauria. They first appeared during the Triassic period, between 243 and 233.23 million years ago(mya), although the exact origin and timing of the evolution of dinosaurs is a subject of active research. They became the dominant terrestrial vertebrates after the Triassic-Jurassic extinction event 201.3 mya and their dominance continued throughout the Jurassic and Cretaceous periods. The fossil record shows that birds are feathered dinosaurs, having evolved from earlier theropods during the Late Jurassic epoch, and are the only dinosaur lineage known to have survived the Cretaceous-Paleogene extinction event approximately 66 mya. Dinosaurs can therefore be divided into avian dinosaurs—birds and the extinct non-avian dinosaurs, which are all dinosaurs other than birds.

The eras of the Colossals:
Era Period Time Span (Million Years Ago) Key Features
Mesozoic Triassic 252 - 201 First dinosaurs and mammals appeared; dry climate with deserts.
Mesozoic Jurassic 201 - 145 Age of giant sauropods; lush vegetation; first birds evolved.
Mesozoic Cretaceous 145 - 66 Flowering plants appeared; T. rex and Triceratops roamed; ended with mass extinction.

Dinosaur Mummy Fossil

This rare fossil preserves not only the bones but also the skin and soft tissues of a dinosaur, offering an incredible glimpse into its real appearance. Found mostly frozen.

Dinosaur Skeleton Fossil

Complete or partial dinosaur skeletons give paleontologists valuable insight into anatomy, posture, and movement—revealing how these giants once lived and thrived.

Ammonite Fossil

Ammonites were spiral-shelled marine mollusks that lived millions of years ago. Their fossils are among the most beautiful and recognizable in the world.

Dinosaur Footprint Fossil

Trace fossils like dinosaur footprints reveal how these creatures moved, hunted, and interacted with their environment millions of years ago.

Amber Fossil

Amber is fossilized tree resin that sometimes traps ancient insects, plants, or air bubbles, preserving them perfectly for millions of years.

Petrified Wood

Petrified wood forms when minerals replace the organic material of trees, preserving their structure in stunning stone detail over eons.
